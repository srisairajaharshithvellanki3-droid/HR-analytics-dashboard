I built an HR analytics dashboard in Power BI on a 1,104-employee dataset — and tried to make it answer one question instead of just displaying numbers: what story is this workforce actually telling?

Three things stood out:

→ Gender split sits at roughly 60/40 (male/female) — a meaningful gap worth tracking, not an extreme one.

→ Tenure shows a settled workforce, not a churny one — very few brand-new hires, but solid numbers all the way out to 9 years. Recent hiring looks like it's slowed down.

→ The org is bottom-heavy: ~82% of employees sit at Job Level 1–2, with only a thin slice at Level 4–5. That's the real story — are tenured employees actually climbing, or plateauing?

Built with Power BI + DAX (CALCULATE, DIVIDE measures for gender %, headcount cards, and category breakdowns by tenure and job level).

The technical part is easy. The harder part is asking what the dashboard is actually arguing — not just what it's displaying.
